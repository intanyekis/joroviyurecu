How did Julio Iglesias start his Professional Career?

After a serious car accident that rendered him immobile for several years, Julio’s tenure as a goalkeeper came to an end.

He needed the guitar, which a nurse gave him, to play to move his fingers while recovering from the accident, and ever since, he has realized his ability. After winning the 1968 Benidorm International Song Festival, Julio decided to advance his profession.

Yo Canto, the debut record by Julio, was a huge hit when it was released in 1969; it peaked at number one on the Spanish charts and briefly occupied third position.

He continued to issue numerous albums, but the most popular and well-received are Soy from 1973, A Flor De Piel from 1974, El Amor from 1975, De Nia a Mujer from 1981, and 1100 Bel Air Place from 1984, among others.
